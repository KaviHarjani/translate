Blokada bloque moins de publicités.

Si vous suspectez que Blokada bloque moins de publicités qu'avant, rafraîchissez vos listes s'il vous plaît. Pour ce faire, appuyez sur Liste noire, puis en haut à droite sur le menu, choisissez Rafraîchir les filtres et appuyez sur Continuer. Habituellement, cela se produit parce que de nouveaux serveurs sont ajoutés aux existants, lesquels ne sont pas (encore) identifiés et ajoutés à des listes noires, ou bien, lorsque les dernières listes ne sont pas encore téléchargées sur votre appareil, cela explique pourquoi les publicités ne sont pas (encore) bloquées.

Alternativement, vous pouvez ajouter plus de listes d'hôtes ou ajouter des filtres plus restrictifs dans le menu Liste noire.

Une application ne fonctionne pas correctement.

Si vous pensez qu’une application ne fonctionne pas correctement, qu'elle ne charge pas son contenu ou qu'elle ne se comporte pas de manière habituelle. Allez à l’écran principal de Blokada, choisissez Liste Blanche, tapez sur le signe " + ", choisissez App et tapez son nom. Tapez sur Enregistrer à la fin. En effectuant cette manipulation, Blokada ne filtrera plus les URLs pour cette application.

Je souhaite bloquer un site particulier.

Vous pouvez bloquer de manière individuelle un site qui n'est pas dans la liste d'hôtes. Pour ce faire, allez dans Liste noire sur la page principale, tapez sur le signe + en bas de l'écran, tapez sur Hôte et tapez le nom d'hôte du site Web, sans les parties http et www (par exemple : monexemple.com).

Je ne parviens pas à démarrer Blokada après l'installation.

Il existe deux causes courantes qui peuvent expliquer une telle situation. Tout d’abord, vous pouvez vérifier si vous avez une connexion VPN active dans les paramètres du téléphone, et si l'option VPN toujours activé est activée. Pour ce faire, accédez aux réglages de votre téléphone, recherchez VPN dans la section réseau, tapez sur la roue à côté du VPN existant. Désactivez-le, déconnectez la connexion VPN active et essayez de redémarrer Blokada.

Si vous n’avez pas d'autre connexion VPN active, vérifiez si Blokada est réglé sur En ligne uniquement. Si oui, vérifiez si vous êtes bien connecté à internet via wifi ou via données mobiles. Blokada ne démarrera pas si l'option En ligne uniquement est activée et que vous n’avez pas de connexion internet active.

Si cela n’aide pas, redémarrez votre téléphone, cela résout parfois le problème.

Blokada arrête de fonctionner/ne marche plus après un moment.

Première chose à faire, veuillez activer l'option Garder en vie sur l'écran d'accueil de Blokada. Dans ce cas, Blokada va essayer de rester en vie, mais veuillez noter que votre système peut toujours tenter de contourner cette option et tuer Blokada en arrière plan. Si vous avez effectué ce réglage et que vous vous rendez compte que Blokada s'arrête tout de même après un moment, vérifiez les paramètres de votre système Android et désactivez toutes les options d'optimisation de mémoire ou de batterie pour l'application Blokada. Malheureusement, ces options sont implémentés différemment chez chaque constructeur, c’est pourquoi nous ne pouvons pas détailler de méthode générale pour le faire. Nous vous recommandons de parcourir les paramètres de votre appareil. Une autre source de problème peut se trouver dans le fait que vous utilisez une appli "Nettoyeur de mémoire" . Dans ce cas là, veillez à exclure Blokada en le mettant sur liste blanche ou sur la liste d'exclusion du nettoyeur. Si aucune des étapes reprises ci-dessus ne vous a aidé, veuillez signaler un bug (voir plus bas comment faire).

I can't import EasyList/AdBlock Plus list into Blokada.

If you try to import a list (for example EasyList) that works very well with your AdBlock Plus extension on your PC or notebook, Blokada will report an error message. It is because Bloakda uses other method to block ads. While ABP blocks and hides elements or parts of the website, Blokada cannot do it because it's not a plugin in the browser, instead it redirects the accesses to the ad-server to a non-routable address (0.0.0.0), therefore it needs a hostname-IP list.

How does Blokada work?

Blokada is a thin layer between the network interface (wifi or mobile) of your phone and the application/s you use. To be able to filter every outgoing traffic, it generates a VPN connection and simply blocks any requests towards the hosts defined in the blacklist filters. This blocking is done with a redirection, the IP address of the website that serves the advertisement will be resolved by the phone to a non-routable meta-address, for 0.0.0.0, where no web service is running, therefore nothing will be shown. With this, Blokada saves both your data plan and battery life. The filters are updated automatically and contain hosts known for serving ads, tracking, spam, malware, and other unwanted or dangerous content. You can configure Blokada with your own custom filters and whitelists to ensure the service that you would like to use isn’t blocked and you don’t experience any interruption.

Can I use another VPN while Blokada is turned on?

Unfortunately no, it is a system limitation; you can have only one active VPN connection at a time. If you join to an other service, Blokada will exit.

What are the options in Blokada?

As you surely saw it, Blokada has some options on the main screen that are not detailed at the first start. Please let us do it now. You can see the following:

  • Blacklist: this is the lists of host files containing the servers to block. Please note that using multiple lists can have effect on network and system speed.
  • Whitelist: the whitelist is the exception list, here you can define hosts that should not be blocked or apps where Blokada won’t filter URLs. Blokada is installed with several whitelisted apps by default.
  • Keep alive: this option is to ensure Blokada is not paused or stopped by the system. Please note your system can overwrite it, make sure your settings will let Blokada run in the background.
  • Notifications: when enabled, you will be notified when an ad is blocked. It is on by default, but if you get a lot of notifications, turn it off.
  • Online only: when enabled, Blokada will exit if there is no active connection, and start again whenever connection comes back. If disabled, Blokada will always run in the background (even if no network is available). Please note this option may not work on every device.
  • Start on boot: when enabled, Blokada will start after the system boots up. Please note there are system level configurations for this option, therefore it cannot be guaranteed Blokada can start on boot. In this case, please check your phone’s settings. The default configuration is enabled, if you want to start Blokada manually, disable this option.
  • DNS: if you are suspecting that some websites or services are blocked for you, or you are presuming that your internet provider collects information about your intenet usage, choose a secure DNS machine from the list. By default, Blokada uses the same DNS server/s as your system.

Not configurable things:

  • Up to date: if you see it, you are running the most recent version of Blokada. Keep the app up-to-date because it has less bugs and more features!
  • # ads blocked: the number of unwanted content that Blokada blocked since it is started for the first time
  • # hosts: number of servers that serves advertisements, malware and other unwanted content

What can I do if I still see some ads?

Some ads will come through with any ad blocker on Android. In particular YouTube ads or some fullscreen popup ads are difficult to block. If you think more ads are coming through than before, try these steps:

  • Check if the VPN key icon is present in the status bar at the top. If not, start Blokada.
  • Open Blokada and check if it is active and not reporting any errors.
  • Check if the hosts counter is not equal to zero. If it is, select some blacklist filters so that ads can be blocked. If the counter is still zero, report a bug.
  • Make sure you don't have too many apps in the whitelist - maybe that's why you are seeing ads.
  • Try resetting your filters (go to the Blacklist screen and choose Restore Defaults from the Quick Actions menu at the top).
  • If you see ads in your browser, deactivate any kind of traffic compression or optimisation in the browser's settings.
  • Deactivate Blokada, close it (by swiping it out from the Recents screen), and turn it on again.
  • If you recently changed your settings (filters, whitelist, etc), remember it takes time before new configuration start working. Give it some time.
  • Clear app data for Blokada (in system settings), or reinstall it.
  • Restart your device.
  • Your device may be unsupported. Please check Devices Database.
  • If none of those steps helped, please report a bug (see below).

What is DNS and which should I choose?

The acronym DNS stands for Domain Name System and this servers are responsible to resolve the domain names (for example: myexample.com) to IP addresses, what are used between machines to communicate. Your internet service provider has its own DNS servers; Blokada is configured to use the system defaults. The machines in this list should be used if you are suspecting that your ISP collects logs about your internet usage, you are afraid of some privacy risk or if some websites or services are blocked for you. There is no "best", everything depends on your preferences and location, therefore if you would like to use custom DNS servers, please read their description, search for them on the internet and read the feedbacks.

I cannot install an update, what can I do?

First of all, please check if the Android version you have is 5 or newer. Android 4 and older versions are not supported in Blokada 3.0 and newer versions. To do it, go to the Settings of your phone, scroll to About phone and search for Android version

If your phone runs Andriod 5 or newer, please restart your device. It sounds strange, but sometimes it helps. If that didn't solve the issue, uninstall Blokada and install it again. It can be, that your device is unsupported. Please check Devices Database.

If none of those steps helped, please join to our Telegram chat on go.blokada.org/chat to get help, or report a bug (see below).

Can I hide the VPN notification?

Unfortunately no. Some devices may be displaying annoying VPN notification (a key icon) while VPN is active, and it is not possible to hide it. If you can, try updating your device to Android 8 (Oreo), since newer Android versions do not have this status report.

Can Blokada see my private data?

Non. Blokada does not read or tamper with your private information, it only monitors which hosts your device is making requests towards and blocks the ones matching the filters. Every time Blokada blocks anything, you will see a notification (what you can turn off). Blokada has no access to your encrypted traffic.

Does Blokada use a lot of battery?

Non. Depending on your settings the battery impact is minimal, and on most new devices, especially on Marshmallow (Android 6) or newer, you will not notice any battery drain. However, some unlucky devices may experience increased battery drain. Refer to the Devices Database for details.

Will Blokada slow down my device?

No. Depending on your settings Blokada can be quite efficient. In fact, both your device's performance and battery life may visibly improve, because Blokada saves your device from downloading and processing content you don't need or care about. However, users with some older devices may experience performance drop. Refer to the Devices Database for details.
